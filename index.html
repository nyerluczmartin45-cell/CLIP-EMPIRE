Core Skills
THE ART OF THE CUT Core

Editing is not just cutting — it's rhythm, emotion, and engineering attention. You'll learn the professional system for mining viral moments out of long-form content (2-hour podcasts, Twitch VODs, lectures, interviews), then assembling them into tight, punchy shorts that hold 90%+ retention all the way to the final frame.

Viral Moment Detection: Scan transcripts for emotional peaks — laughter, controversy, surprising facts, bold opinions. These 5 categories drive shares and can be found in any long video in under 5 minutes
The Hook Formula (First 3 Seconds): The OPEN loop technique — start mid-sentence, use a bold claim, show the ending first, or ask an irresistible question. Never waste the first frame on intros, logos, or "hey guys"
Pacing & Cut Timing: Hard cuts every 2–4 seconds for fast-paced content, J-cuts and L-cuts for smooth interview edits, and how to use silence as a dramatic tool. The "no dead air" rule and when to break it deliberately
Emotional Architecture in 60 Seconds: Structure every clip with a mini arc — hook → tension → payoff. Even a 30-second clip should feel like a complete experience with a satisfying resolution
Reframing 16:9 → 9:16: Smart pan-and-scan techniques, auto-reframe in CapCut, and how to use B-roll cutaways to hide awkward aspect-ratio moments without cropping the speaker's face
Music Sync & Sound Design: Royalty-free music sources (Epidemic Sound, Artlist, YouTube Audio Library), how to cut on the beat, and using whoosh transitions and sound effects to punctuate moments without overpowering the speaker
The 80/20 Edit Workflow: Why 80% of your edit decisions should be locked on the first pass — and how to avoid the endless re-editing trap that destroys your output as a working editor

Visual Identity
CAPTIONS, GRAPHICS & STYLE Visual

Most editors cut. Great editors brand. This module teaches you how to build a complete visual identity — dynamic captions, color grading, graphic overlays, and animated text — that makes your edits instantly recognizable and keeps viewers glued even with sound completely off. Visual storytelling is your highest-leverage skill.

Caption Systems That Retain: The 3 caption styles dominating 2026 — word-by-word highlight (MrBeast style), bold bottom-third bars, and kinetic subtitle pops. When to use each one and why the wrong font can kill retention
Caption Design Science: Font hierarchy (thick display fonts at 80–120px), high-contrast color pairings, animated word emphasis, and the "active word" highlight effect that increases watch time by 15–30% according to A/B tests by top Shorts channels
Color Grading for Short-Form: The 3 go-to LUT styles — punchy/saturated (lifestyle), moody/cinematic (motivational), and clean/bright (educational). How to apply and customize LUTs in CapCut, DaVinci, and Premiere in under 2 minutes per clip
Kinetic Text & Motion Graphics: Creating pop-in titles, animated lower thirds, and text overlays using CapCut's built-in library and free After Effects templates from Motion Array and Envato
B-Roll & Stock Footage Integration: Free sources (Pexels, Pixabay, Coverr), paid sources (Storyblocks), and how to match B-roll to the spoken word so visuals reinforce — rather than distract from — the message
Building a Client Template System: Create reusable project presets for each client: their brand fonts, colors, intro bumpers, and caption styles. Deliver a consistent look at scale without starting from scratch every edit
The Silent Viewer Test: Every edit must communicate its full value with the phone muted and the screen sideways. If your video fails this test, it fails the algorithm — here's your 5-point checklist to fix it before export

AI Tools
AI-POWERED EDITING WORKFLOWS Advanced

AI has permanently changed the editing landscape. Editors who use AI tools produce 5–10x more output in the same time — which means you can take on more clients, charge more, and deliver faster. This module builds a complete AI-assisted pipeline from raw long-form video to finished, captioned, exported short — without sacrificing creative quality.

Opus Clip Deep Dive: How the "virality score" algorithm works, how to configure your clip preferences, and how to batch-process an entire YouTube channel's back catalog into shorts in one session. Pro tip: always manually review the top 3 clips before sending to clients
Descript Transcript Editing: Edit video by editing words — delete a sentence in the transcript and the video cuts automatically. Overdub AI voice for seamless audio fixes. The fastest way to clip podcast content at professional quality
CapCut AI Suite: Auto-captions with speaker detection, voice isolation from noisy environments, AI smart trim (removes silences and filler words), background removal without green screen, and the AI text-to-video generator for B-roll creation
Runway Gen-3 & kling AI: AI video enhancement (upscale low-quality footage to 4K), background replacement, motion blur addition, and generative B-roll — create cinematic visual elements from a text prompt and cut them into your edit
ElevenLabs & AI Voiceover: Clone a client's voice for consistent narration across their content, generate AI voiceovers for faceless channels, and add professional narration to clips without the client needing to record anything new
Building the Full AI Pipeline: Long video → Opus Clip extracts moments → Descript cleans audio → CapCut AI adds captions and color → export to all 3 platforms simultaneously. This pipeline can process 10 finished shorts per hour with practice
The Human-AI Balance: AI handles the mechanical 70% — you own the creative 30%. Knowing when to override the algorithm, when to add a manual transition, and when the AI's suggested clip actually misses the emotional peak that a human would catch

Trends
RIDING TRENDS LIKE A PRO Strategy

Trends are free distribution. Knowing how to spot, adapt, and execute a trend before everyone else is the competitive advantage that gets your client's content in front of millions — not thousands. This module teaches trend intelligence as a daily professional system, not luck or guesswork. You'll understand how the algorithm thinks and how to feed it exactly what it rewards.

How the Algorithm Works in 2026: YouTube Shorts uses a two-stage distribution model — a small test audience first, then a massive push if watch completion is high. TikTok's interest graph vs. YouTube's social graph — why the same content performs differently and how to optimize for each
Trending Audio Intelligence: How to find trending sounds on TikTok's Creative Center and Instagram's Reels tab before they peak, use them inside 48 hours of their trend window, and legally layer them under client content (fair use vs. licensing rules)
Format Playbook — The 8 Formats That Work in 2026: Fast-cut montage, talking head + B-roll, reaction clip, before/after reveal, list format ("5 things no one tells you..."), story arc, documentary-style, and silent visual storytelling — with examples and templates for each
The Vertical-First Principle: Shooting philosophy, editing safe zones (avoid placing text above 85% or below 15% of the frame), thumbnail optimization for the Shorts shelf, and how to use the full 9:16 canvas with graphic elements that boost visual density
Hook Engineering in 2026: The 5 proven hook formulas — Contrast Hook ("Most people do X. Wrong."), Curiosity Hook ("Here's why top creators never do this..."), Stakes Hook ("I lost $50,000 because of this"), Social Proof Hook, and Direct Address Hook. Testing two hooks per clip and tracking which wins
Retention Analytics: Reading the audience retention graph in YouTube Studio, identifying where viewers drop off, and reverse-engineering those drop points to fix pacing, hooks, or transitions in future edits. This is how you get clients results fast

Business
LANDING CLIENTS & GETTING PAID Business

You can be the best editor in the world and still be broke if no one knows you exist. This is the business module — the one that separates hobbyists from professionals. You'll learn how to build a client-ready portfolio from scratch, find and close paying clients within 30 days, price your services correctly, and build a sustainable, growing monthly income from your editing skill.

Portfolio From Zero: Create 5–10 spec edits using free YouTube content from public creators. Choose a niche (fitness, business, motivation, comedy) and build a portfolio that positions you as a specialist — not a generalist. Specialists earn 40–60% more
Where to Find Clients (Ranked by Conversion Rate): (1) Twitter/X DMs to creators with 10K–500K followers, (2) YouTube comment sections offering a free sample edit, (3) Reddit communities (r/editors, r/youtubers, r/entrepreneur), (4) Upwork and Fiverr for inbound leads, (5) LinkedIn for corporate/brand clients at premium rates
The Cold DM Framework That Converts: 3-line DM structure — Personalized compliment (specific, not generic) → Problem identification (their content is inconsistent or their Shorts underperform) → Proof + offer (link to sample edit of THEIR content). Response rate: 15–25% when executed correctly
Pricing Architecture: Beginner rate: $15–25/clip or $300–500/month retainer (10 clips). Mid-tier: $30–50/clip or $800–1,500/month. Advanced: $75–150/clip or $2,000–5,000/month packages for major brands. Never charge per hour — charge per deliverable
Client Onboarding Process: Discovery call template (30 min), brand intake form, content access setup (Google Drive/Frame.io/Dropbox), revision policy (2 rounds max), payment terms (50% upfront for new clients), and delivery SLA (48–72 hours turnaround)
Getting Testimonials & Case Studies: After month 1, email your client a 3-question survey. Turn their answers into a case study with before/after metrics (views, subscribers gained). Post it on your social — this becomes your best sales asset
Raising Your Rates & Firing Bad Clients: Rate increase script for existing clients, how to identify clients who drain your time vs. pay your worth, and a graceful 30-day exit strategy that protects your reputation while freeing your capacity

Scale
SCALING TO FULL-TIME INCOME Scale

Getting your first client is a milestone. Building a business that runs without you is a transformation. This module teaches you how to systemize your editing workflow, delegate overflow work, diversify your income across multiple streams, and transition from "freelancer" to "agency owner" — so you're building an asset, not just trading time for money.

SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures): Document every repeatable task in your workflow — clip selection criteria, export settings, delivery naming conventions, caption style per client. A well-documented SOP lets you hand work to a subcontractor without a single Zoom call
Managing 5+ Clients Simultaneously: The batching method — dedicate Monday to all client intakes, Tuesday–Thursday to editing blocks, Friday to delivery and feedback. Use ClickUp or Notion with a client board, status tags, and auto-reminders so nothing falls through the cracks
Launching Your Faceless Shorts Channel: Pick a niche with existing demand (finance facts, history moments, dark psychology, motivational speeches). Use public domain or properly licensed content. Monetize via YouTube Partner Program ($3–$10 per 1,000 Shorts views), affiliate links, and sponsorships. This becomes passive income while you sleep
UGC (User-Generated Content) for Brands: UGC creators earn $100–$800 per video creating raw, authentic-looking product content for brand ads. You don't need followers — you need your face (or a stand-in) and editing skill. Platforms: Billo, Trend.io, JoinBrands, direct brand outreach on LinkedIn
Hiring Your First Subcontractor: When you're at 80% capacity, hire a junior editor at $8–15/clip. Use Upwork, Contra, or your own network. Train them using your SOPs. You now earn the margin between what you charge and what you pay — this is your agency model taking shape
Digital Product Income: Create and sell CapCut template packs, Lightroom/DaVinci LUT bundles, transition packs, or mini-courses on Gumroad or Payhip. Price at $17–$97. With an audience of 2,000 followers, a $47 product launch can generate $2,000–$10,000 in a single week
The $5,000/Month Breakdown: 4 retainer clients at $800/month = $3,200 · 1 UGC brand deal = $600 · Faceless channel AdSense = $400 · Template sales passive = $800. This is achievable within 6–9 months of consistent execution. Your roadmap starts now
